A Tile-based TF2 Map Maker



 

What is the Mapper?

     Coded entirely in python, the mapper is an extremely powerful tool that allows users to easily create a map fully compatible with TF2 in a matter of seconds. Directed at both amateurs and Hammer veterans, this program eliminates the learning curve of Hammer and also speeds up the map-making process for those people, respectively. Features include:

  • Construct simple maps with the press of a few buttons
  • Use prefabs to construct world geometry
  • Create your own prefabs for use in the program

Now you can create your very own TF2 map without any previous knowledge of Hammer. Currently in release 1.0.2

How Does It Work?

    Maps are created with "blocks" each 512x512 hammer units big. Every time you click a button on the grid in the work area, it places a block down with whatever prefab you have selected in the prefab list. Once you layout your map in the work area grid, you can either save it as an EZ Mapper File (*.ezm) to work on it later, or export it as a Valve Map File (*.VMF) that you can then open up with hammer. Then, you have the option of editing the map even further (by applying textures, patching up any fine details), or compiling the map for play in Team Fortress 2.

You can find the mapper at http://github.com/baldengineers/easytf2_mapper. Instructions are located on the wiki for how to operate it.

    We are the Bald Engineers, a group of two Team Fortress 2 players that wanted to leave a lasting impact in the TF2 community (and hopefully obtain a community weapon in the process). Thus emerged the innovative and brilliant "Easy TF2 Mapper". Although the name may sound cheesy, it is an extremely powerful tool that can create a map fully compatible with TF2 in a matter of seconds. While we aren't playing TF2, you will probably find us developing one of our newest programs. Currently, we only have one program: The Easy TF2 Mapper. If you would like to follow our programs, please check out our Github page.

    How did this all start anyhow? Well, the two team members, both self-taught in the programing language of python, combined both of their strengths, technicality, practicality, and efficiency, to conceive a program extensive in its simplicity and usefulness. Although countless debates raged on about good coding practices between the two, in the end, their teamwork weaved the intricate puzzle pieces into place to complete the mapper.
